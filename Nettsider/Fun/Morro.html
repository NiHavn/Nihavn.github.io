VISIT OSLO

About Oslo

The capital of Oslo is one of Europe’s fastest-growing cities. International media is constantly writing about Oslo’s innovative architecture, museums, and neighbourhoods, as well as everything that moves on the food, fashion, art, and music scenes. Over the last few years, several new quarters have been established in the city centre, with exciting new activities and attractions. The high-rise buildings in Barcode and captivating landmarks like the Opera House and the Astrup Fearnley museum are changing the face of ' the city, and will soon be accompanied by a new Munch Museum and the new National Museum.

PLACES TO VISIT

The opera building

Part of the Sentrum area, Aker Brygge is known for its pier, where eateries with outdoor tables serve upscale Nordic and French cuisine, or casual fare like burgers and steak. A popular summer boat bar is moored nearby, and ferries depart year-round for the scenic Oslo Fjord.

The Frognerpark

The Frognerpark is one of Oslos many parks that you can visit to get a glance at some of the finer sculptures Oslo has to offer. Along with its sculptures it makes for a good place to clear your thoughts and smell the good smell of flowers during summer.

PLACES TO EAT

Restaurants

There are many restaurants spread throughout Oslo, some more exclusive than others so we are sure you will find a plase fit for your liking. Anything from anywhere in the world, you name it, Oslo has it!

Sushi

Visit a sushi restaurant to get your hands on some fine exotic foods and maybe some dessert to top the cake!

Hamburger

Most restaurants will serve burgers for you and your friends, so make to visit a restaurant if hamburgers are your pasion!
